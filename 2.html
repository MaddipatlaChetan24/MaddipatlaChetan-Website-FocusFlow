Productivity Basics

To-Do List

A to-do list is a tool for organizing tasks you need to complete. It’s typically a list of items—written or digital—that you prioritize and check off as you finish them, helping you manage time and stay focused.

Pomodoro Timer

A Pomodoro timer is a time management method created by Francesco Cirillo. It uses focused work sessions (usually 25 minutes, called a "Pomodoro") followed by a 5-minute break, with a longer break (15-30 minutes) after four cycles. It enhances productivity by structuring work into short, concentrated bursts.

Note-Taking

Note-taking is the act of writing down or recording important information, ideas, or insights during activities like studying, meetings, or planning. It aids memory retention, organizes thoughts, and provides a reference for later use.

Goal Setting & Tracker

Goal setting is the process of identifying specific, measurable objectives you aim to achieve, often with deadlines. A goal tracker is a tool—digital or physical—that tracks your progress toward these goals, keeping you motivated and accountable.
